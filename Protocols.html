This page contains protocols that our lab uses. Helpful for when writing papers as well as when taking notes (eg "see Database>>Protocols>>Pull Downs")

Ideas

  • collapsible protocols
  • dated and intialed
  1. Take cells out of -80 C freezer and put onto ice to thaw slowly (~20 min)
    1. Place onto not into ice to speed up the process
  2. Set heat block to 42 C
  3. Take plasmids out of -20C freezer
    1. If lyophilized, resuspend in 40 uL ddH2O
    2. If hydrated, thaw (okay to thaw quickly)
  4. Add 5 uL plasmid to 100uL cells
    1. Return plasmids to -20C
  5. Place cells on ice for ~30 min
  6. Heat shock for 45 sec at 42 C
  7. Place back on ice for ~2 min
  8. Add 1 mL SOC media
  9. Incubate for 1hour in tabletop shaker at 37C, 210 rpm
    1. Tape in sideways to best aerate
  10. Take new agar plates out of fridge (2 per transformation). Label around the edges of the side WITH the agar with: protein name, cell type, antibiotic, 900 or 100 uL, date, + initials
  11. Remove cells from the incubator after 1 hour
  12. Pipet 100 uL onto “100 uL” labelled plate, spread with 5-7 glass beads
    1. Return glass beads to “Dirty Glass Beads” jar
  13. Spin down remaining cells in small tabletop centrifuge for 60 sec at 12000 rpm
  14. Remove ~800 uL of supernatant (eject into biohazard)
  15. Pipet 100 uL onto “900 uL” labelled plate, spread with 5-7 glass beads
  16. Place plates into stationary incubator overnight
    1. Plan to remove them the next day

    Next day...

  17. If moving directly into overnights, see overnight protocol
  18. Parafilm plates and place in white fridge
